1. Renting House Guideline

Any member, who intends to sublet his / her Flat, has to apply to Society for permission by complying with the following conditions

As per the AGM, All the resident passed the bill with majority that, flat/row house should be rented to family tenant ONLY Click here to download (ZIP File 1.7 MB) the AGM Resolution and Confirmation Letter from Co-Operative Housing Register

A letter by the member to the Society indicating his / her intention to sublet his / her Flat on rent and the reason for the same

Appendix FORM 28 of the Bye Laws duly filled by the member Click here to Download

Appendix FORM 11 of the Bye-Laws duly filled by the Licensee

Copy of the Stamp Duty paid & Registered Lease agreement

If the Flat is held jointly, both the members have to sign the lease agreement in Membership order.

Personal Memoranda of the Licensee in the enclosed format

If, the Licensee is a Corporate Body / Firm, then a letter from them informing the occupants of the Flat with their personal memoranda Letter of Undertaking

Member should clear the outstanding dues if not cleared already

Tenant verification from the local Police station

Obtain No Objection Certificate from the Society before the tenant moves in Click here to download the NOC Format

Member should submit any valid ID proof of the Tenant and his family member who will be staying with him

Society should be informed before the tenant moves IN/OUT

Power of Attorney in absence of Flat owner, please keep the copy of Pan Card/passport/voter card for identification

Introduction of Tenant by owner of the Flat to any one of the committee member

After seeking No objection Certificate from the Society only member can keep tenant in the Flat.

Owner or Tenant should not request for entry / exit of the goods without prior documentation /permission at the last minute.

Model bylaws for Sub - letting etc. of Flats. Page no.19, section 42 onwards. Click here to Read - Sub - letting of Flats

In case a Licensee occupies the Flat without prior permission, then it will be treated as illegal occupancy and the Society reserves the right to take further action in the matter.. Member's co-operation is Solicited
